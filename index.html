Research

Research Proposal PhD Direction

Mitochondrial Genetics in Immunity and Neurodegeneration

My proposed doctoral direction asks how mitochondrial genetic variation and genetically predicted mitochondrial functional capacity may influence antiviral immunity, inflammation, and long-term neurodegenerative risk.

  • Focus on mtDNA SNVs, haplogroups, heteroplasmy, and mtDNA copy number.
  • Integrate viral infection RNA-seq with immune-response phenotypes.
  • Use cohort-scale genetics, GWAS, polygenic scores, and Mendelian randomisation.

Research assistant project at Shanghai Jiao Tong University using R, Python, and Linux to analyze pediatric hepatoblastoma transcriptomic data.

  • Analyzed bulk RNA-seq and single-cell RNA-seq datasets.

03/2024-11/2024 MSc Dissertation

Computational Prediction of Optimal Protein Tagging Sites

MSc dissertation project at the University of Edinburgh using R and Python to evaluate computational predictions of protein tagging preferences.

  • Worked with large-scale mutational datasets and sequence-derived features.
  • Benchmarked predictions using correlation analysis and ROC/PR evaluation.
  • Examined enrichment patterns, including GTPase-related proteins, sequence length, and intrinsic disorder.
